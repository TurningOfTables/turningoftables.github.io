One of the most damaging elements of traditional software development culture has been a strict allocation of duties between disciplines. “The designer draws some pictures and describes some functionality, the developers write code to make those pictures, the testers check that it works as expected.” That might work where projects aren’t especially time constrained, and the business is comfortable making slow progress. Stop Making Testing Boring

It really doesn’t have to be. I’ve had the pleasure of interviewing a lot of different software testers over the years and many can talk interestingly and intelligently about testing. There is a certain subset of testers that just make me want to quit testing though. Primarily they have a background working in large companies with significantly sized test teams. Over the years as I’ve been a part of numerous web development projects which lack test automation. One recurring theme is the idea that test automation is a silver bullet. “Test automation will allow us to check hundreds of items in seconds”, goes the thinking. “Once we create a test suite I can always just press a button to be assured of quality”, it continues.
